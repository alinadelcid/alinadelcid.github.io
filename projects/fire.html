OVERVIEW

Redesigning to meet brand goals

The Fire Museum of Maryland is a non-profit institution located in Lutherville, Maryland. Originally founded in 1971, it is now home to a world-class collection of historic hand engines and fire apparatus. To help grab the attention of the museum’s target audience, younger children as well as older adults, a redesigned identity was created to capture the essence of the museum. They sought a rebrand that honored their history but provided a stronger emphasis on their mission to educate the public on the history, technology, and innovation of the U.S. fire service.

LOGO

Logo Design

The logo brings together ideas of historical apparatus and honorable service by illustrating a historic firetruck in the foreground and a golden badge in the background. The visualization of the firetruck was inspired by a real vehicle in the museum, specifically the 1957 Seagrave “Anniversary Series” Pumping Engine. The badge helps convey the sense of safety and honor that is often associated with fire departments.

BRAND GUIDELINES

Guidelines for Brand Visuals

To protect the integrity of the logo and maintain brand consistency, logo rules were created. Additionally, brand colors and typography specs were outlined.

WEBSITE

Website Redesign

The redesign of the website focused on improving navigation and UX by prioritizing a clear user flow, sufficient color contrast (according to WCAG accessibility standards), responsive line and section spacing, and clear call-to-actions. This major redesign aims to improve the UX of the brand's target audiences, including older adults.
